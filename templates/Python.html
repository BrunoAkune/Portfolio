Canadian Violence Dashboard

Excel analysis of violent crime rates across Canadian provinces

The Problem

Understanding the distribution of violent crime across Canada is essential for informed policy decisions and resource allocation. This project analyzes provincial crime statistics to identify which regions face the highest rates of violence, providing a foundation for deeper analysis into specific areas of concern.

My Role

I collected and analyzed Statistics Canada crime data using Excel, creating an interactive dashboard with pivot tables, charts, and conditional formatting to visualize violent crime rates by province. The analysis revealed key insights about regional crime patterns and temporal trends.

The Impact

  • Key Finding - In 2018, Ontario was the province with the highest crime rate in Canada, highlighting the need for focused analysis on this region
  • Data-Driven Direction - This discovery directly led to my ML Crime Forecast project, where I applied machine learning to predict future crime trends specifically in Toronto, Ontario
  • Actionable Insights - The dashboard enables stakeholders to compare crime rates across provinces and identify areas requiring targeted intervention

Crime Analysis

Toronto Police Service crime data analysis (2013-2025)

The Problem

This project addresses the need to better anticipate crime levels in Toronto by forecasting future monthly offence counts for a selected neighbourhood using historical Major Crime Indicator data from the Toronto Police Service. The goal is to build and evaluate a supervised machine learning model that, given past reported incidents and temporal features (year, month, day-of-week), predicts upcoming crime volumes to support more data‑driven planning and resource allocation.

My Role

responsible for acquiring and cleaning Toronto Police Service crime data, performing exploratory data analysis, engineering time‑based and neighbourhood‑level features, building and tuning a regression model to forecast monthly crime counts, and evaluating model performance with appropriate error metrics and visual diagnostics.

The Impact

  • Data Cleaning - Addressed missing and inconsistent values, ensured unique event identification, and processed geographic and categorical fields for accurate analysis
  • Trend Analysis - Quantified crime volume changes over time, revealing a 65% increase in reported offenses from 2014 to 2023; identified the years with minimum and maximum criminal incidents
  • Forecast - Shows how monthly crime forecasts at the neighbourhood level could help with planning patrol coverage, prioritizing higher‑risk periods, and informing community safety initiatives, aligning with how predictive analytics are used for resource allocation in policing.

Crime Statistics Dashboard

Tableau visualization for commercial building safety planning

Crime Statistics for Opening Commercial Buildings in Toronto

This dashboard analyzes the crime trends in Toronto from 2013 to 2024. It analyzes all the crimes reported in commercial buildings helping stakeholders to understand the dangers they might encounter, common time stamps crime occur and the most dangerous neighboorhoods historically.

The Problem

The Company is looking to expand their business and are interested in opening a new physical retail store in Toronto. However, they need to understand the crime statistics in Toronto to plan for a security and loss prevention budget.

My Role

Independently completed data preparation, visual design, and analytical insights using Tableau to analyze crime trends across Toronto neighborhoods, time periods, and crime categories for commercial building safety planning.

The Impact

Successfully identified crime trends across all neighborhoods. Showcasing which categories are prominent and time periods, showcasing the ability to derive business insights from complex datasets.

Convenience Store Robbery Analysis

GIS spatial analysis for public safety planning

The Problem

Law enforcement and urban planners needed to understand spatial patterns of convenience store robbery incidents to allocate resources effectively and develop targeted crime prevention strategies for high-risk areas.

My Role

Created a professional GIS map using ArcGIS to analyze convenience store robbery patterns, identifying geographic hotspots and spatial clustering for law enforcement and public safety planning applications.

The Impact

Delivered actionable spatial intelligence through cartographic visualization, enabling stakeholders to identify crime hotspots, prioritize patrol routes, and implement evidence-based crime prevention measures in high-risk commercial areas.
